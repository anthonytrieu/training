## Week-by-week detail

### Week 1 (Jul 20–26) — break the stop-go habit
- **Tue**: 15 min warm-up, then **3×15 min @ 245–260 W** (sweet spot), 5 min easy between. No stops mid-interval — pick a route that allows it (e.g. UBC loop, Iona flats, or Stanley Park at a quiet hour).
- **Thu**: 60–75 min with **30 min continuous @ 218–240 W** (tempo). Cadence 85+.
- **Sat**: **70 km / ~800 m** at steady Z2 (170–210 W), minimal stops. Include one 20+ min climb at tempo — Cypress to the first lookout or Seymour half-way works.
- **Sun**: 60 min easy Z1–Z2.

### Week 2 (Jul 27–Aug 2) — extend
- **Tue**: **2×20 min @ 250–265 W**, 8 min easy between.
- **Thu**: 75 min with **2×20 min @ tempo** on rolling terrain.
- **Sat**: **85 km / ~1,000 m**. Full Cypress or full Seymour in the middle, climbed at 220–245 W. Start practicing race fueling: 60–80 g carbs/hour, drink every 15 min.
- **Sun**: 75 min easy.

### Week 3 (Aug 3–9) — biggest build week
- **Tue**: **2×20 min @ 265–285 W** (threshold). Hard but repeatable; if the second one collapses, the first was too hard.
- **Thu**: 90 min hilly tempo, ~45 min total in Z3.
- **Sat**: **100 km / ~1,300 m** — e.g. Vancouver → Horseshoe Bay → Lions Bay and back, which puts you on the actual event road. Ride the climbs at goal fondo pace (220–250 W).
- **Sun**: 60–75 min easy. You should finish this week tired.

### Week 4 (Aug 10–16) — recovery (do not skip this)
- Cut volume ~40%. **Tue**: 1×20 min @ tempo only. **Sat**: 60 km easy, flat, social pace. Everything else Z1–Z2 or off the bike.
- This is the week fitness actually consolidates. HRV and sleep should rebound; if they don't, extend the easy days.

### Week 5 (Aug 17–23) — peak 1
- **Tue**: **3×20 min @ 250–265 W**, 5–8 min between — this is the signature fondo workout; 60 min of sweet spot is excellent specificity.
- **Thu**: 75 min with **5×3 min @ 305–330 W** (Z5 touch-up), full recoveries. Keeps the top end awake without big fatigue.
- **Sat**: **110 km / ~1,500 m** at target event pace. Treat it as a full rehearsal: same breakfast, same fueling, same pacing discipline (do NOT surge above 260 W on climbs).
- **Sun**: 75 min easy.

### Week 6 (Aug 24–30) — peak 2 + dress rehearsal
- **Tue**: **2×25 min @ 255–270 W**.
- **Thu**: 60 min, 30 min tempo, otherwise easy.
- **Sat**: **Dress rehearsal, 120–130 km / 1,500–1,800 m.** Ideal: ride the actual course section Horseshoe Bay → Squamish (→ turn back), or Vancouver → Squamish one-way with a pickup. Full event nutrition, event clothing, event start time (~7 AM). This ride answers every remaining question.
- **Sun**: recovery spin only.

### Week 7 (Aug 31–Sep 6) — sharpen, start shedding fatigue
- **Tue**: **2×15 min @ 270–285 W**.
- **Thu**: 60 min with 20 min tempo.
- **Sat**: 80 km moderate with ~800 m — comfortable, not hard.
- **Sun**: 60 min easy. Volume this week ≈ 60–70% of week 6.

### Week 8 (Sep 7–12) — taper and race
- **Mon**: off. **Tue**: 60 min with **3×8 min @ 260 W** — enough intensity to stay sharp. **Wed**: 45 min easy. **Thu**: off or 30 min spin. **Fri**: 30 min with 3×1 min race-pace openers; prep bike, bottles, drop bag; **in bed by 10 PM**.
- **Sat Sep 12 — race day.** Start conservatively: the first hour out of Vancouver is fast and adrenaline-heavy — sit at 190–210 W even though it feels trivially easy. Ride climbs at 220–250 W, never above 260 W before Squamish. Eat from minute 20: 60–90 g carbs/h, ~500–750 ml fluid/h (more if hot). The ride is decided on the false-flat grind from Squamish to Whistler (last 50 km, steadily uphill) — that's where the watts you saved early come back as places gained.

## The three things that matter most

1. **No-stop discipline.** Your average ride has ~35% stopped time. Every key session (Tue/Thu/Sat) must be continuous riding. This alone converts your existing fitness into fondo-usable fitness — and it will pull your Garmin VO2 max back up as a side effect.
2. **The Saturday long ride.** If life forces you to drop sessions, drop anything but this one. Progression 70 → 85 → 100 → 110 → 125 km is the spine of the plan.
3. **Sleep regularity.** You averaged two poor nights (< 5 h) per week recently with bedtimes swinging three hours. A fixed ~11 PM bedtime is worth more than any single workout in this plan. Watch the Jul/Aug pattern: your worst rides follow your worst nights.

## Morning self-check (no watch, so do this manually)

Before each key session: resting HR within ~5 bpm of your ~50 baseline, legs not sore from daily life, motivation present. Two or more missing → swap the session for the easy alternative (Z2 spin, same duration). One bad day never breaks a plan; three ignored ones do.

## Adjusting the plan

- **Feeling flat mid-build**: insert an extra rest day; drop Sunday, never Saturday.
- **Missed a week entirely**: repeat the last completed week rather than skipping ahead.
- **Sweet spot intervals feel easy by week 5** (HR < 150 at 260 W): your FTP has moved — nudge targets up ~5 W, or re-test with a 20 min max effort on a Tuesday.
- All watt targets assume the single-sided meter's readings are representative; pace by feel + HR (tempo ≈ 140–155 bpm for you) whenever power looks implausible.
